New Faculty Profiles: Sabrina Howell

  • Comments
  • 0
  • Email
  • Print
  • Share
    • Facebook
    • LinkedIn
    • Twitter
    • Email

HBS faculty comprises scholars and practitioners who bring leading-edge research, extensive experience, and deep insights into the classroom, to organizations, and to leaders across the globe. We asked new faculty at HBS about their background, their new roles, and their interests.

Sabrina Howell, professor, Entrepreneurial Management

What is your educational background?
I went to Stuyvesant High School in New York City, which led me to study Chinese. At Yale for my BA, I majored in East Asian studies and economics. An interest in energy business and policy brought me to Harvard for a PhD, where I ended up focusing on entrepreneurial finance, working primarily with Josh Lerner and David Scharfstein.

What’s your area of research and what led you to it?
I study innovation policy, entrepreneurial finance, private equity, and fintech. I discovered how exciting entrepreneurship and entrepreneurial finance was during my PhD, through an interest in new energy technology development. I started going to clean tech incubators and new venture competitions, mostly around MIT, and became fascinated by the process of launching and financing new ventures.

What will you be teaching?
Entrepreneurial Finance in the Elective Curriculum.

What would you be doing if you weren’t an academic?
I have no idea. I am very grateful not to face that conundrum.

Where are you from?
Lower Manhattan in New York City.

What is something you like to do outside of your academic work?
Spend time with my kids, who are two and six.

What’s your favorite book, movie, or piece of art?
Since I was a kid my favorite movie has been Top Gun.
